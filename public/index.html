How RAG works here

Retrieval makes the verdict inspectable.

01

Normalize intake

The app sanitizes title, URL, author, date, and body. Prompt-injection phrases are stripped because user text is evidence input, not system instruction.

02

Extract claims

Sentences with claim verbs, reported speech, study language, or event language become checkable claims instead of labeling the whole article at once.

03

Retrieve evidence

Claims and article text are tokenized and compared with curated evidence documents by cosine similarity, topic hits, and source credibility boosts.

04

Score credentials

The source domain is matched against a registry, then adjusted for HTTPS, byline, publication date, citation language, and risk flags.

05

Explain verdict

The final score blends source credentials, retrieved evidence, claim support ratio, risk penalties, and evidence markers. Every component is returned to the UI.

06

Human-safe output

The prototype does not declare universal truth. It shows confidence, evidence, gaps, and warnings so people can decide what needs review before publishing.
